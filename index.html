Our Story

“She's your lobster. C'mon you guys. It's a known fact that lobsters fall in love and mate for life. You can actually see old lobster couples, walking around their tank, you know, holding claws. ... See? He's her lobster!” - Phoebe Buffay, The One With The Prom Video, Friends

Shishir and Sonya met at West Lafayette High School in 2006. Most of you know the story, they went to many high school dances together and may have had a date where Sonya refused to kiss him! In 2008 Shishir started college at Purdue and in 2009 Sonya started college at Indiana University.

After a few years without connecting, they met again in summer of 2011 when Shishir was visiting Bloomington for a conference. Later that year, Shishir spent a semester in Ireland for study abroad, but they continued to talk across the ocean through Skype. After Shishir returned, they finally realized what they mean to each other. Sonya started medical school in 2013 at IU-West Lafayette while Shishir was completing his Master’s from Purdue, so they got to spend a year together in the same city.

In July 2014 Shishir moved to Lexington to start his PhD in Biology at The University of Kentucky. They went back to frequent Skype chats and a 4-hour drive to see each other every few weeks. It was a hard few years through medical school but soon, Sonya finds out she matched at UK for residency! Life in Kentucky has been what they had wanted for a long time. In June of 2017, they become pawrents by adopting their furbaby girl, Zoey. They got engaged in Nashville in March of 2018 and they cannot wait to get married and celebrate with their friends and family in October!

Wedding Details

Our wedding and reception will take place at The Loudoun House in Lexington, KY.

“The Loudoun House is on the National Register of Historic Places and is one of only five remaining castellated Gothic Revival villas left in the United States designed by New York architect A. J. Davis. Built in 1850 for Francis Key Hunt, cousin of Francis Scott Key, who wrote the U.S. national anthem and the 10th child of John Hunt, one of the first millionaire's west of the Appalachians, The Loudoun House is now owned by the city of Lexington and has been the Lexington Art League's administrative and curatorial home since 1984.”

We chose this location after attending an event there and imagining ourselves enjoying the beautiful house with all of you. As the sun sets the windows cast a pretty, golden glow on everything the light touches. We cannot wait for you to experience it!

Parking at the Loudon House is reserved for catering. We recommend taking an Uber or Lyft, however street parking is available in the neighborhood surrounding the venue if you choose to drive yourselves to the wedding.
